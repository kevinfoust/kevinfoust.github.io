I have been an artist since I was a child and photographer since about 1984.  Back in the day (80s) I learned photography with an Olympus OM PC with a Zuiko 50mm f/1.8 lens as my first 35mm camera.  That was a highly automated affair and was good for developing some idea about how to take a photograph. I photographed all over the place, although I didn't carry the camera all the time as I almost do now.

I've been through experimental phases when I only shot with Polariod film.  Making image transformations with Time-Zero film was a real passion of mine.  Emulsion transfers were also a favorite; I love the delicate nature of emulsion floated on to a sheet of water color paper. 

Many years later I still lean towards experimental image making but now with either a digital camera or 35mm film cameras.  My work has been trending more abstract for a few years and to me it feels like it is going where it should.  Of course, there is the danger in visual arts of speaking a language that no one else understands.  I'm trying to tread close to that line without falling into complete mad abstraction that maybe I don't even understand.

I was born in Illinois, lived in Missouri, then Southern California and now Europe.  Each of those moves has informed my art in differing ways. Missouri was rooted in developing basic photography skills and struggling to photograph what was in front of me in interesting ways.  I wasn't too abstract in those days.  Moving to Southern California in 1996 unleashed the Polaroid years of experimentation and continuous improvements.  Slowly I moved back into more conventional imagemaking with digital cameras again in the 2010s and began photographing people and shooting street photographs.  The street has proved not so productive for imagemaking but it did dial in my skills  for visualizing and photographing with intention.  And it also taught me that shooting on the street without an intention is not a good us of my time.

Moving closer to now, timewise, imagine composites occupy a good bit of my thinking and I shoot with a composite in mind.  I'm finding my into this and finally producing some images that I like.
