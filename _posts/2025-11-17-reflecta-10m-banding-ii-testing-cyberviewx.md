# Solving Reflecta 10M Banding: CyberViewX Gave Me Cleaner Scans
When I reached out to [Reflecta](http://reflecta.de) about the banding issue on my Reflecta RPS 10M film scanner, they quickly lost interest once they learned I wasn’t using CyberViewX their OEM software. I’ve been running SilverFast 9 Ai, so I assumed that wouldn’t matter. Apparently, it does.

Their position made me think: Why not try CyberViewX? I hadn’t bothered before, figuring the banding would show up regardless of the software. Then I could at least return to Reflecta with proof and get some help. To their credit, they did respond promptly to my initial email.

Installing CyberViewX on my iMac was easy. And much to my surprise, the results were eye-opening.

[![](https://kevinfoust.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/CyberViewX_nobanding-1024x681.jpg)](https://kevinfoust.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/CyberViewX_nobanding-scaled.jpg)*CyberViewX Scan*

[![](https://kevinfoust.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/CyberViewX_nobanding-closeup.tif)](https://kevinfoust.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/CyberViewX_nobanding-closeup.tif)*CyberVueX closeup.  No banding, at least none that I see.*

It turns out scanner performance is highly dependent on the software. I had assumed the application simply issued commands to the scanner’s firmware to trigger the same mechanical process each time. Wrong. Even the sounds the scanner makes differ between SilverFast and CyberViewX, which tells you the scan routines aren’t the same at all.

For now, the banding issue sits squarely in SilverFast’s court. It’s been over a week since I opened a support ticket and I haven’t heard a word back. That seems to be the downside of their “pay for support” model.

Note to self: Software matters—a lot more than I expected.
