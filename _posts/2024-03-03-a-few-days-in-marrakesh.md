# A few days in Marrakesh
We traveled to Marrakesh; a first excursion into Morocco. Spending five days there was a bit much.  As a photographer it is a challenging place. The locals around the medina are strongly anti-photo, anti-photographer. They really don't want to be photographed.  Locals will demand a payment for a photo.  At some level I understand; the proliferation of the cell phone snap and social media has commoditised images to the extent that the people start feeling objectified and part of a profit making scheme in which they receive no share.

Photographing around the area, I accepted [the challenge of excluding people from my photo](https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/2013/nov/17/marrakech-magnum-photography)s. Working with the contrast between the archaic and new (not necessarily modern) I started to find a vision. The chaos, crowds, and energy would normally feed my photography.   Here with the constraints of no people in photos it took a lot of time to bring the vision. Then constant badgering by hawkers, touts, and grifters made focusing on making images subject to constant distraction.

Marrakesh in eight photos.
